In an environment when deposit and bond yields are so poor, they are a great way to earn reasonably safe income.<>Dividend yield is a measure of the amount of dividends a company pays out relative to its stock price. It is calculated by dividing the annual dividend per share by the current market price per share. Dividend yield can be an important factor to consider when evaluating a stock, as it can provide insight into the company's financial health and the level of return that investors can expect to receive from their investment.There are several reasons why dividend yield can be an important factor to consider when evaluating a stock:Income: Dividend yield can provide a steady stream of income for investors, particularly for those who rely on dividends as a source of income.Financial health: Companies that consistently pay dividends are generally financially stable and have a track record of profitability. Therefore, a high dividend yield may indicate that a company is financially healthy and has a strong ability to generate profits.Return on investment: Dividend yield can be a key factor in determining the overall return on an investment in a particular stock. A high dividend yield can provide a higher level of return for investors, especially if the stock price remains relatively stable.It's important to note that dividend yield is just one factor to consider when evaluating a stock. Other factors such as the company's financial health, growth potential, and market conditions can also impact the attractiveness of a particular stock.
