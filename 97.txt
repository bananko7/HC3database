Revenue is the total amount of money that a business generates from the sale of goods or services. Gross profit, on the other hand, is the difference between a company's revenue and the cost of goods sold (COGS). It represents the profit a business makes after accounting for the direct costs associated with producing its goods or services.For example, if a business sells an info product for $100 and the cost of goods sold (COGS) is $50, the gross profit would be $50 ($100 revenue - $50 COGS). Gross profit is an important metric because it shows the profitability of a business before accounting for other expenses, such as marketing, rent, and salaries.To calculate gross profit, you would use the following formula:Gross profit = Revenue - COGSIt's important to note that gross profit is not the same as net profit, which is the profit a business makes after all expenses, including taxes, have been accounted for.<>finance
