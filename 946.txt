Cash would go to CDs at 1-5 year maturities to maximize interest, but keep money maturing every 6-9 months.  Even with the goal of simplifying, my wife and I each have a 401(k), an IRA, and a Roth IRA, I also have an inherited Roth, and I manage my teen's Roth and brokerage accounts. Here are some benefits of using multiple bank accounts:Separation of funds: You can use different bank accounts to separate your funds for different purposes. For example, you could have one account for your daily expenses, one for long-term savings, and one for specific financial goals like a down payment on a house.Better interest rates: Different bank accounts may offer different interest rates. By using multiple accounts, you may be able to earn more interest on your savings.Simplified budgeting: Having separate accounts for different purposes can make it easier to track your spending and stay within your budget.Reduced risk of fraud: If one of your accounts is compromised, you won't lose all of your funds. You can also use different accounts for different types of transactions to reduce the risk of fraud.It's important to keep in mind that using multiple bank accounts may come with additional fees and responsibilities, such as maintaining a minimum balance or paying account fees.
