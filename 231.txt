S&P did have the info available years ago, but no longer that I can find.<>The Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) is a stock market index that tracks the performance of 30 large publicly traded companies listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and the NASDAQ. The companies included in the DJIA are selected by the editors of the Wall Street Journal and are intended to represent the broad market for large publicly traded companies in the United States. The DJIA is considered a bellwether for the overall stock market and is often used as a benchmark for the performance of the stock market as a whole.The NASDAQ 100 is a stock market index that tracks the performance of the 100 largest non-financial companies listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange by market capitalization. The index includes a diverse range of companies in various industries, including technology, healthcare, and consumer goods.The S&P 500 is a stock market index that tracks the performance of 500 large publicly traded companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States. The companies included in the S&P 500 are selected by Standard & Poor's, a financial services company, and are intended to represent the performance of the broad market for large publicly traded companies in the United States. The S&P 500 is often used as a benchmark for the overall stock market and is widely followed by investors and analysts.It is important to note that the membership of these indices can change over time as the companies included in the index are reevaluated and added or removed based on various criteria such as market capitalization and financial performance.
